This figure illustrates the dynamic response of an aircraft's stability and control characteristics over time, specifically focusing on various parameters during a maneuver. The horizontal axis represents time in seconds, while the vertical axis displays multiple variables, each with different units.

Key variables shown include:

1. **Acceleration Factor, \(A_z\)**: Indicates the vertical acceleration experienced by the aircraft.
2. **Normal-Force Coefficient, \(C_N\)**: Reflects the aerodynamic lift characteristics.
3. **Pitching Velocity**: Measured in radians per second, this shows how quickly the aircraft is pitching.
4. **Elevator Control Force, \(F_e\)**: Measured in pounds, it represents the force applied to the elevator.
5. **Airplane Angle of Attack, \(a\)**: Measured in degrees, this indicates the angle between the chord line of the wing and the oncoming airflow.
6. **Tail Angle of Attack, \(a_t\)**: Also in degrees, this shows the angle at which the tail surfaces interact with the airflow.
7. **Elevator Angle, \(\delta_e\)**: The angle of the elevator control surface, affecting pitch.
8. **Horizontal Tail Load, \(L_t\)**: Measured in pounds, indicating the load on the horizontal tail.
9. **Mach Number, \(M\)**: Represents the speed of the aircraft relative to the speed of sound.
10. **Dynamic Pressure Ratio, \(q/q\)**: A dimensionless ratio indicating the dynamic pressure experienced by the aircraft.
11. **Change in Average Aileron Angle, \(\Delta \delta_a\)**: The change in the aileron angle, measured in degrees.

The figure provides insights into how these parameters evolve during a specific maneuver, highlighting the interdependencies between control inputs (like elevator angle and force) and the resulting aerodynamic responses (like angle of attack and tail load). Students should focus on how changes in control inputs affect the aircraft's stability and response over time, an essential concept in aircraft design and control systems.
