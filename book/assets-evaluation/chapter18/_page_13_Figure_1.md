### Variables and Axes
- **Total Velocity ($V$)**: Represented as a vector pointing outward, indicating the overall speed of the aircraft.
- **Axes**: 
  - **X-axis**: Horizontal axis pointing to the right.
  - **Y-axis**: Vertical axis pointing upwards.
  - **Z-axis**: Axis pointing towards the observer, typically representing the lateral direction.
  
### Key Components
- **Velocity Components**:
  - **$U_B$**: Component of velocity along the X-axis.
  - **$V_B$**: Component of velocity along the Y-axis.
  - **$W_B$**: Component of velocity along the Z-axis.
- **Angles**:
  - **$\alpha$**: Angle of attack, which affects lift and drag.
  - **$\beta$**: Sideslip angle, indicating the angle between the aircraft's body axis and the relative wind.

### Physical Phenomenon
The figure illustrates how the total velocity vector can be decomposed into its components along the aircraft's body axes. This decomposition is crucial for analyzing aerodynamic forces acting on the aircraft, as it helps in understanding how changes in orientation (angles $\alpha$ and $\beta$) affect the aircraft's performance and stability.
