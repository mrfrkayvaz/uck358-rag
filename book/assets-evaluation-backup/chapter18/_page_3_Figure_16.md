- The \(Z\) axis is the vertical axis.

The angles depicted are:

- \(\alpha\): Angle of attack, which is the angle between the chord line of the wing and the oncoming airflow.

- \(\gamma\): Roll angle, which indicates the rotation about the longitudinal axis.
- \(\mu\): The yaw angle, representing the rotation about the vertical axis.

These angles are crucial for understanding the aircraft's stability and control characteristics. The key engineering insight is that these angles affect the aerodynamic forces acting on the aircraft, influencing its maneuverability and stability.
