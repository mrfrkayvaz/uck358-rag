This figure illustrates the aerodynamic characteristics of an airfoil, focusing on key points relevant to stability and control. The main variables depicted include:

- **U**: The free-stream velocity, indicating the direction of airflow over the airfoil.
- **α (alpha)**: The angle of attack, which is the angle between the chord line of the airfoil and the oncoming airflow.
- **θ (theta)**: The pitch angle, representing the angle of the airfoil relative to a reference line.
- **c**: The chord length of the airfoil, which is the distance from the leading edge to the trailing edge.
- **G**: The center of gravity of the airfoil, which is crucial for determining stability.
- **ec**: The distance from the aerodynamic center to the center of gravity, which influences the moment about the pivot point.

The **zero-lift line** indicates the angle of attack at which the airfoil generates no lift. The **aerodynamic center** is a point along the chord where the pitching moment remains constant regardless of the angle of attack, typically located at the quarter-chord point for subsonic airfoils.

The key engineering insight from this figure is the importance of the relationship between the center of gravity and the aerodynamic center. If the center of gravity is located ahead of the aerodynamic center, the airfoil will tend to be stable, while if it is behind, the airfoil may become unstable. Understanding these relationships is crucial for designing stable aircraft.
