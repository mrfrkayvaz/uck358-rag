This figure illustrates two different wing geometries: a straight tapered wing and an elliptical wing. It focuses on key parameters that influence the aerodynamic properties of these wing shapes, particularly the mean aerodynamic chord (MAC) and the centroids of the wing sections.

### Variables and Concepts:
1. **Wing Geometries**:
   - **Straight Taper**: The wing has a constant taper ratio from the root chord \( c_r \) to the tip chord \( c_t \).
   - **Ellipse**: The elliptical wing shape is shown, which is often considered optimal for minimizing induced drag.

2. **Key Parameters**:
   - \( c \): The overall chord length at the root.
   - \( c_r \) and \( c_t \): The root and tip chord lengths, respectively.
   - \( b \): The wingspan, with \( b/2 \) indicating half the wingspan.
   - **Centroid**: The location of the centroid for each wing shape is marked, which is crucial for understanding the distribution of aerodynamic forces.

3. **Mean Aerodynamic Chord (MAC)**:
   - For both wing shapes, the MAC is indicated, which is essential for stability and control analysis. The MAC is defined as the chord that would produce the same lift as the actual wing shape.

- The figure does not explicitly label axes, but the dimensions are typically in meters (m) or feet (ft) for wing geometries.
- The MAC is a critical parameter in aerodynamic calculations, often expressed in the same units as the chord lengths.

### Key Engineering Insight:
Understanding the relationship between wing shape and aerodynamic performance is vital for aircraft design. The straight taper and elliptical shapes demonstrate how geometry influences the center of pressure and overall lift distribution. The MAC serves as a reference for stability calculations, affecting the aircraft's handling characteristics. Designers often aim for an optimal wing shape to reduce drag and enhance performance, particularly in terms of lift-to-drag ratio.
