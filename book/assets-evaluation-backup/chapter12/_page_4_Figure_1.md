The figure from Chapter 12 of "Airplane Stability and Control" presents two graphs relevant to engine performance in relation to thrust and control inputs.

### Top Graph: Engine Lag vs. Engine Thrust
- **Axes**: The x-axis represents Engine Thrust (in pounds, lb), while the y-axis shows Engine Lag ($T_E$, in seconds).
- **Description**: This graph illustrates the relationship between engine thrust and the time lag in engine response. As thrust increases from 0 to approximately 10,000 lb, the engine lag decreases sharply from about 4 seconds to around 1 second. 
- **Key Insight**: The rapid decrease in lag with increasing thrust indicates that higher thrust levels lead to quicker engine response times. This is crucial for pilots to understand, as it affects how quickly they can expect the aircraft to respond to throttle changes.

### Bottom Graph: Engine Thrust vs. Power Lever Angle
- **Axes**: The x-axis shows the Power Lever Angle ($\delta_{PLA}$, in degrees), while the y-axis indicates Engine Thrust (in lb).
- **Description**: This graph depicts a non-linear relationship between the power lever angle and the thrust produced by the engine. Initially, there is a gradual increase in thrust with the lever angle, followed by a steeper increase as the angle approaches 70 degrees.
- **Key Insight**: The non-linear response emphasizes the importance of precise control inputs, especially in the mid-range of the power lever angle, where small adjustments can lead to significant changes in thrust. Understanding this relationship aids in optimizing engine performance during various phases of flight.

In summary, these graphs highlight the dynamics of engine response and control, emphasizing the importance of thrust levels and control inputs in aircraft operation.
