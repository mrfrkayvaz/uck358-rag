The figure illustrates the control surface configuration of an aircraft, specifically focusing on the wing and tail surfaces. Key components include the ailerons, flaperons, spoilers, elevators, and rudder, which are essential for maneuverability and stability.

### Variables and Components:
- **Ailerons (OB AIL)**: Located on the outer wings, these control roll about the longitudinal axis.
- **Flaperons**: Act as both ailerons and flaps, providing lift and roll control.
- **Spoilers (OB SPOILERS)**: Positioned on the wings, these devices disrupt airflow to reduce lift and increase drag, aiding in descent and roll control.
- **Elevators (Left and Right)**: Control pitch, affecting the aircraft's nose up or down attitude.
- **Rudder**: Located on the vertical stabilizer, it controls yaw, helping to steer the aircraft left or right.
